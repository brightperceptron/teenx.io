About TeenX

Who We Are TeenX is a youth-led non-governmental organization based in Dhaka, Bangladesh. Since its founding, TeenX has established itself as a leading voice in youth protection, child trafficking prevention, and community-based safety networks. Unlike traditional NGOs, TeenX is uniquely positioned: our leadership team consists primarily of young people (ages 16-35) who have directly experienced or witnessed the challenges facing vulnerable youth in Bangladesh. This lived experience informs every program decision, every community partnership, and every policy recommendation we make. We operate in all 64 districts of Bangladesh through our network of trained Youth Envoys—teenagers and young adults who serve as protection coordinators, awareness educators, and community liaisons in their own neighborhoods.
Our Founding Story TeenX was founded by Shahi Al Sadat in response to a personal tragedy that highlighted the failure of existing systems to protect vulnerable teenagers. Witnessing the inadequacy of institutional responses to trafficking cases, Shahi envisioned a different model: what if we empowered teenagers themselves to be the first line of defense in their communities? The founding principle was revolutionary in the NGO space: teenagers are not just victims to be protected, but agents of change who can protect each other. This youth-centric approach has become TeenX's defining characteristic. From a single district operation in 2022, TeenX has grown to operate in all 64 districts of Bangladesh, with plans to expand to South Asia by 2030.

Mission & Vision

Our Mission TeenX actively works to prevent child trafficking and teenage rape by building awareness, protection networks, youth-led action, and rapid support systems for vulnerable teenagers across Bangladesh. In operational terms, this means: • **Prevention through Education**: Comprehensive awareness training reaching teenagers, parents, and communities about trafficking methods and prevention strategies. • **Protection Networks**: Building and maintaining district-level networks of trained youth who monitor threats and provide peer support. • **Rapid Intervention**: Operating 24/7 emergency response systems to help victims of trafficking or sexual violence. • **Community Accountability**: Creating systems where community members are empowered to identify and report trafficking risks without relying solely on government institutions. • **Policy Advocacy**: Using evidence collected through our networks to influence national policy on child protection and victim support.
Our Vision A world where no child is trafficked and no teenager becomes a victim of rape — a society where young people are protected, communities are vigilant, and justice stands stronger than exploitation. This vision encompasses: • **Universal Youth Protection**: Every teenager in Bangladesh has access to peer-led protection networks and rapid support systems. • **Community Vigilance**: Every community has the knowledge, capacity, and systems to identify and respond to trafficking threats. • **Justice Systems**: Law enforcement and judicial systems are equipped to prosecute traffickers and support victims comprehensively. • **Regional Leadership**: The TeenX model becomes the standard for youth protection across South Asia. • **Youth Agency**: Young people are recognized as essential partners in protecting their own communities, not just as passive victims.

Youth Protection Network

What is the Youth Protection Network? The Youth Protection Network is TeenX's core program: a distributed, community-based system of trained young people who work as protection coordinators in their own neighborhoods. Rather than relying solely on government institutions or external NGOs, the Youth Protection Network empowers local teenagers and young adults to be the first line of defense against trafficking in their communities. This network is currently active in all 64 districts of Bangladesh, with 1,000+ trained envoys working in schools, transportation hubs, markets, and communities.
How It Works **Finding Threats:** Envoys are trained to identify trafficking risk factors, warning signs, and vulnerable individuals. They monitor key locations known to trafficking networks and report suspicious activities to senior coordinators. **Building Awareness:** Envoys conduct peer-to-peer education in schools, neighborhoods, and youth groups. They normalize conversations about trafficking and teach prevention strategies. **Rapid Response:** When identified, at-risk teenagers are connected with support systems. Suspected trafficking is reported to law enforcement and TeenX's emergency response team. **Community Care:** Envoys provide ongoing support to survivors in their communities, helping them rebuild lives and reconnect with families. **Data Collection:** Each envoy contributes to TeenX's national trafficking database, informing prevention strategies and policy advocacy.
